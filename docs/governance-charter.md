# Guardian Academy Governance Charter

**Version:** 0.1  
**Effective Date:** March 2026  
**Status:** Draft  

---

## 1. Mission & Principles

**Guardian Academy** develops open standards and tools for measuring institutional integrity through the Guardian Integrity Score (GI²).

### Core Principles

1. **Transparency:** All standards, metrics, and methodologies are publicly available.
2. **Independence:** Governance decisions are made by independent stakeholders, not single actors.
3. **Evidence-Based:** All metrics are grounded in measurable, auditable data.
4. **Accountability:** Trustees are subject to the same standards they promote.
5. **Inclusivity:** Diverse perspectives inform standard evolution.

---

## 2. Governance Structure

### Roles & Responsibilities

#### **Founder/Executive Director**

**Authority:**
- Day-to-day operations and strategic direction
- Hiring and staffing decisions
- Budget allocation (within board-approved amounts)
- Public representation of the organization

**Accountability:**
- Reports quarterly to the Board of Trustees
- Subject to GI² audits (must score ≥ 60)
- Cannot have conflicts of interest exceeding thresholds in §6

**Constraints:**
- Cannot unilaterally change core standards (requires Trustee approval)
- Cannot spend > $50,000 without Finance Committee approval
- Cannot enter contracts > $100,000 without Board approval

---

#### **Board of Trustees**

**Composition:**
- Minimum 5 members, maximum 9 members
- At least 60% independent (no board members from same organization)
- Diverse across: sector, geography, expertise, demographics
- Term limits: 3-year terms, maximum 2 consecutive (6 years)

**Authority:**
- Approve annual budget and strategic plans
- Elect Executive Director
- Approve changes to core standards (GI² metrics, formula)
- Hire and fire Executive Director
- Approve contracts > $100,000
- Establish governance committees

**Accountability:**
- All Trustee decisions logged and published (quarterly)
- Board minutes available to stakeholders within 30 days
- Annual external audit of governance compliance

**Decision-Making:**
- Quorum: 4 of 9 trustees
- Standard decisions: Simple majority (≥ 50% + 1)
- Major decisions (new metrics, IP changes): Supermajority (≥ 2/3)

---

#### **Chief Auditor (Independent Role)**

**Authority:**
- Conducts GI² audits on the organization itself
- Reviews governance compliance
- Reports directly to the Board (bypassing Executive Director)
- Can investigates allegations of misconduct

**Independence:**
- External contractor, not employed by Guardian Academy
- Selected by Board, removal requires supermajority vote
- Compensation set by Finance Committee (independent review)

---

#### **Finance Committee**

**Composition:**
- 3 Trustees with finance expertise
- Minimum 2/3 independent from any grant source

**Responsibilities:**
- Monthly budget review and cash flow forecasting
- Audit oversight (commission, scope, followup)
- Approve all expenses > $50,000
- Set Executive Director compensation
- Ensure financial controls and transparency

---

#### **Standards Committee**

**Composition:**
- 3-5 Trustees + external experts
- Mix of institutional leaders, academicians, practitioners

**Responsibilities:**
- Review proposed changes to GI² metrics
- Evaluate new standards or frameworks
- Manage stakeholder feedback on standards
- Publish standards roadmap annually

## 3. Decision-Making Processes

### Standard Setting (GI² Metrics & Formula)

**Proposal Phase:**
1. Stakeholder consultation (30-day public comment period)
2. Standards Committee review and recommendation
3. Board presentation with pros/cons analysis

**Approval Phase:**
1. Board discussion (at least 2 meetings)
2. Supermajority vote (≥ 2/3) required
3. 30-day appeal period for stakeholders
4. Final Board ratification

**Documentation:**
- All proposed changes published 60+ days before vote
- Rationale and evidence for each change documented
- Version history maintained; old standards archived

---

### Budget & Spending

**Annual Budget:**
1. Finance Committee proposes budget (December)
2. Board approves (January)
3. Executive Director executes within approved limits
4. Monthly reporting to Board

**Spending Authorities:**
- < $5,000: Executive Director
- $5,000–$50,000: Finance Committee approval
- $50,000–$100,000: Full Board vote
- > $100,000: Board vote + external legal review

---

### Conflicts of Interest

**Disclosure Requirements:**
- All Trustees and executives must disclose:
  - Board memberships
  - Financial interests > $10,000 in related orgs
  - Family relationships with staff/trustees
  - Contracts or grants from Guardian Academy

**Recusal Rules:**
- Cannot vote on matters involving direct financial benefit
- Cannot vote on matters involving conflicts of interest
- Abstentions documented in board minutes

---

## 4. Accountability Mechanisms

### External Audits

**Annual Audit:**
- Independent financial audit (Big 4 or equivalent)
- Governance compliance audit
- GI² self-assessment on organization
- Report published within 90 days

**GI² Score Requirements:**
- Organization must achieve ≥ 60/100
- If score drops below 60, 90-day remediation plan required
- If still below 60 after 90 days, board leadership changes required

---

### Stakeholder Accountability

**Quarterly Reporting:**
- Board meeting summaries (decisions, votes, rationale)
- GI² score of organization with component breakdown
- Financials (budget vs. actual, major expenses)
- Key personnel changes

**Annual Report:**
- Comprehensive review of year's activities
- Financial statements and audit results
- GI² assessment and trend analysis
- Strategic outlook for next 3 years

**Feedback Mechanisms:**
- Email: accountability@guardian.org
- Public comment periods on standards changes
- Annual stakeholder survey on governance effectiveness

---

## 5. Amendments to Charter

**Process:**
1. Proposed amendment published 60+ days in advance
2. Board discussion at ≥ 2 meetings
3. Supermajority vote (≥ 2/3) required
4. 30-day appeal period for stakeholders
5. Final Board ratification

**Exception:** Emergency governance changes (e.g., fraud discovered) can be voted immediately but must be reviewed by external auditor within 30 days.
